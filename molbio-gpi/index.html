GPI anchor deficiencies

GPI-anchor deficiencies are a new subclass of congenital disorders of glycosylation (CDG). Mutations that affect the synthesis of the Glycosylphosphatidylinositoltriphosphate-anchor (GPI-anchor) may result in a variety of syndromic and non-syndromic autosomal recessive intelectual disabilities (ID). Hyperphosphatasia with mental retardation syndrome (HPMRS), also known as Mabry syndrome is caused by mutations in genes of the late GPI-anchor synthesis (PIGV, PIGO) as well as by mutations affecting fatty acid remodeling (PGAP2) (Krawitz et al., 2012; Horn et al., 2011; Krawitz et al., 2010). Patients with Mabry syndrome have a characteristic facial gestalt with hypertelorism and long palpebral fissures, a broad nasal bridge and tip of the nose, a long philtrum and a small upper lip. The distal phalanges are shortened and the nails are hypoplastic. Some of the patients have urogenital and gastrointestinal malformations, a muscular hypotonus and seizures. The elevated serum alkaline phosphatase is a characterstic feature in all HRMPS cases.

We offer on a research basis a GPI-anchor synthesis pathway screen. This gene panel includes the following genes: DPM1, DPM2, DPM3, GPAA1, GPLD1, MPDU1, PGAP1, PGAP2, PGAP3, PIGA, PIGB, PIGC, PIGF, PIGG, PIGH, PIGK, PIGL, PIGM, PIGN, PIGO, PIGP, PIGQ, PIGS, PIGT, PIGU, PIGV, PIGW, PIGX, PIGY, PIGZ
